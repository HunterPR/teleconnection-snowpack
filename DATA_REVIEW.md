These are generated by:

Recent pipeline expansion:

- Added extended teleconnections from local PSL transforms (`qbo`, `np`, `pmm`, `wp`, `solar`).
- Added nearby monthly SNOTEL predictors (`stampede_*`, `olallie_*`, `tinkham_*`).
- Added external daily predictors from `fetch_external_predictors.py`:
  - `met_daily_features.csv` (Open-Meteo regional weather + freezing-line proxy)
  - `ndbc_multi_daily_features.csv` (multi-station NDBC recent marine context)

## Why This Structure

- Keeps original source files unchanged (safe for audit and backtracking).
- Creates a single Snoqualmie-centered table at a consistent daily timestep.
- Separates target construction from feature engineering.

## Duplicate/Overlap Notes

- `buoy_fixed.csv` and `buoy_1975_present_extended.csv` appear structurally identical.
- `sno_clean.csv` and `sno_clean_cleaned.csv` share schema but differ heavily in row count.
- `olallie_monthly_snotel.csv`, `stampede_monthly_snotel.csv`, and `tinkhamcreek_monthly_snotel.csv` are station-parallel monthly files (not duplicates in content).

## Next Files To Add (Recommended)

For better Snoqualmie predictions:

- Additional RWIS/DOT station observations near I-90 corridor.
- More buoy stations relevant to incoming Pacific storm tracks.
- Hourly or daily freezing-level proxies (e.g., reanalysis or nearby sounding products).

After adding new files, rerun `organize_data.py`.
